Profile

After starting the Engineering Science course at Oxford University, I quickly realised it was not what I wanted to spend my life doing. I have always been fascinated by computers and computing technology, and thought this would be a good way to get involved, unfortunately that was not the case. I decided to leave and instead taught myself to code in HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and Python. I then secured a job developing for a small start up company where I thrived. I helped develop the front end of their website, and enjoyed it so much I decided to join Bristol University to study computer science.

I found a lot of the course extremely interesting, from computer architecture up to high level programming concepts. I gained exposure to languages such as C, Python, and Java, (and some others that I would happily forget), but I was eager to test myself in the real world. So after leaving I used this knowledge to take a position at a business data analytics firm in Bristol.

There I properly experienced real world development which came with plenty of its own lessons. I spent time using python and SQL to manage large datasets and found my feet and working with a team at a large company. I then moved to Birmingham and found a job working as a front end developer for a pet insurance company where I got to grips with Vue and started to really understand JavaScript. I've been enjoying using and learning these technologies while working there but I'm ready to test my skills somewhere new, with fresh challenges and experiences.
